About Me

First system I ever touched was a PS1 with Spyro, Crash Bandicoot. To this day, I remember staring at puzzles that felt impossible and feeling like the walking embodiment of genius the moment I finally cracked one. I fell in love with gaming on my very first session, and honestly, I don't even think I ever beat those games because of access restrictions — but whenever I did get the chance to play, even a hint of a chance, I was hopping on. I was five or six, and yes, I remember that far back. I was addicted then, and I'm still addicted now.

My friend Steve later introduced me to RuneScape, and if I was addicted before, that's when I got strung out — at ten. RuneScape opened the door to online gaming and gave me access to a whole group of like-minded people (gamers, for better or worse). If you calculate the year (2006-ish), yeah… being online at that age was wild.

These days, I play a variety of games I once said I'd never touch, and I don't always play the ones I used to run daily. I've matured a lot — partly because of gaming, and if you play, you just know. Gaming became a part of my growth in a way I didn't understand back then. It was something I loved deeply but never knew how to incorporate into my life in a meaningful way.
